When I Grow Up

When I grow up, I want to be free.
I want to look at the stars and wish it were me,
To dance in the rain, let the cold kiss my skin,
Or be a pilot, slicing through clouds.

I hope I can still say “when I grow up,”
Even when I’m already grown.

I look back at my life—
A graveyard of dreams,
A barren land, infested with doubts.

What will people say?
How would I look?
If only I knew,
There was nothing I could lose.

Is it too late to say “when I grow up?”

Maybe I should go back to painting
And dream of Van Gogh,
Or step into the past,
Barefoot in the sand,
Building castles that crumble with the tide.

But now I am grown,
Crafting castles in air,
And the only thing I can do
Is dream of what could have been.

— Abdishakur Mohammed
