The Pit Inside Me

How do I say how I feel,
When I don’t know how I feel?

Is it fear?
Impending doom?
My hands and feet
As blocks of ice,

Is it my heart racing?
Or skipping a beat?
Or it’s the cold sweat
Trickling down my skin?

I want to breathe,
Slow down and take a breeze,
But my chest feels heavy,
A weight I cannot lift.

“Everything will be okay,”
I lie to myself—
Words as hollow as the pit
Inside me.

Maybe it’s the hollowness inside me,
A dark, never-ending pit,
That swallows everything but grief,
Or feeling like I’m waiting for a bus,
That never comes,

But I can’t turn it off either,
So I’m stuck in this wasteland,
where time stands still,
and every road leads back
to the pit inside me.

— Abdishakur Mohammed
