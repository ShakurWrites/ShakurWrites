Waiting for the Fall

Woke up today and I felt 'nothing.'
There's no weight on my shoulders,
No constant feeling of dread.
There's no panic or anticipation—
The same ones I've felt for days,
Every day when I wake up.
It's supposed to be a relief,
But I don't trust it.

Because I've spent weeks in a tunnel,
Waiting for that bad news.
And just when the load overpowers me,
Just when I am exhausted from waiting,
I wake up—
feeling nothing.

Is it the calm before the storm?
Or the light at the end of a tunnel?
Or maybe—
the light at the end of life?

So I listen harder, waiting,
Because calm never comes without a price.
And I don’t know if it’s better to brace myself—
Or let it take me by surprise.

Or maybe,
I should embrace peace
And not mention its past betrayals.
Maybe today is the day the other shoe drops,
Or maybe it already has—
And I just haven't noticed yet.

— Abdishakur Mohammed
